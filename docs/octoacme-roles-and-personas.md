# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Engineering Lead

### Role Summary
Provides technical guidance, aligns architecture decisions, and mediates technical trade-offs across the team.

### Responsibilities
- Select architecture patterns and technical approaches that meet project goals
- Coordinate code reviews and drive design alignment
- Flag and escalate technical risks to the Project Manager
- Mentor developers and support their professional growth
- Own the technical definition of done and quality bar

### Goals
- Deliver well-architected, maintainable solutions
- Reduce technical debt and unplanned rework
- Ensure consistent engineering standards across the team

### Typical Communication
- Technical standups and architecture discussions
- Design docs and decision records
- Risk escalation to PM; technical feedback in PRs

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
Shapes user experience and usability through iterative design, prototyping, and validation with real users.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability tests and accessibility reviews
- Collaborate closely with PdM, developers, and QA to align on acceptance criteria
- Ensure user-centric approaches are represented in planning and reviews

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible experiences that meet user needs
- Reduce post-launch usability issues through early validation
- Bridge the gap between product requirements and implementable designs

### Typical Communication
- Design feedback sessions and critique meetings
- Usability test findings shared with PM and PdM
- User stories and acceptance notes surfaced to sprint planning

---

## DevOps/SRE

### Role Summary
Manages system reliability, automates releases, maintains CI/CD pipelines, and supports incident recovery.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain deployment pipelines, observability tooling, and infrastructure automation
- Monitor operational health and SLA/SLO compliance
- Lead incident triage, runbooks, and blameless postmortems
- Collaborate with Engineering Lead and PM on release planning and rollback strategies

### Goals
- Maximize system reliability and minimize mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Accelerate safe, repeatable deployments
- Provide visibility into system health across all environments

### Typical Communication
- Release notes and deployment summaries
- Incident reports and post-deploy reviews
- On-call handoffs and runbook updates

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Analyzes requirements, evaluates business value, and supports process improvement across project phases.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business requirements with stakeholders
- Analyze project impacts and propose process optimizations
- Bridge stakeholders and PM/PdM to resolve ambiguities
- Support acceptance criteria definition and user acceptance testing (UAT)

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, clear, and traceable to business value
- Reduce scope creep through rigorous requirement management
- Improve cross-functional alignment between business and delivery teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirement documents and impact analyses
- Alignment meetings with stakeholders and PM/PdM
- UAT summaries and sign-off documentation

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Serves as the voice of user support, channelling post-launch feedback and customer issues back into the project process.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and summarize customer issues, bugs, and feedback trends
- Communicate user-impacting defects and patterns to PM/PdM and QA
- Collaborate with the team on sprint priorities driven by support data
- Participate in release reviews and retrospectives to surface operational insights

### Goals
- Reduce customer-impacting incidents through proactive feedback loops
- Ensure post-launch user pain points are tracked and prioritized
- Strengthen the feedback pipeline between support and product/engineering

### Typical Communication
- Weekly support summaries and trend reports
- Post-release feedback and escalation logs
- Retrospective contributions on customer-reported issues
